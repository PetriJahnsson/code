The meaning of life



Survivor stories

The project


It is almost 70 years since the liberation. With it was the truth about the concentration camps and what was going on there slowly realities to the outside world. With the liberation rolled the so-called white buses with survivors. The organization behind, estimated that the buses rescued 15,000 people of which many ended up in Sweden. Several of them chose to stay and build a new life here.

Personally, I have for years wondered how people manage to endure the exposure of total human humiliation in the ghetto and concentration camps.

We've all read the testimony about the unspeakable treatment people were exposed to in these camps, due to origin or belief or sexual orientation. Not many survivors are alive today. When they are gone, their voices and testimonies are silenced forever.

I want to understand, how, how people manage to move on ... The answer is in the meeting with those who survived! For me it is a question where the answer can not wait.

In our parliament today we have a party with strong national socialist core values and xenophobic views. This means that 5% of the electorate sympathize with these views!

What you see is basically a photo book with pictures taken from primarily Auschwitz - Birkenau. The photos were taken during several trips in the spring, summer and autumn of 2012. The images will be presented along with tales and stories drawn from conversations with survivors.

A book that can bare to "lay around" and be talked about!

We must never forget!

Hans Carlbrand
Born in Gothenburg. Began photographing at age 13 and bought the first system camera at the age of 15 in the mid 70's. Re-Awakened interest in recent years, now with a passion for black and white.

Key words in my pictures today is simplicity and a constant search for the minimalistic. I would call it technology liberated or ascetic if you prefer.


I work with my pictures in projects where parts and components together form a whole, and a story that is worth telling.
